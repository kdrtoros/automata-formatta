How to Use Automata Formatta

Quick Start

  1. Select a citation standard (APA, Harvard, Chicago, MLA, or IEEE)
  2. Paste your manuscript text or upload a .docx file
  3. Click "Audit & Process" to analyze formatting
  4. Review the health report and corrected output
  5. Copy or download your cleaned manuscript

What It Does

✓ Spacing normalization: Standardizes whitespace around statistical operators (=, <, >, etc.)

✓ Statistical symbol detection: Identifies and counts M, SD, p, t, F, n, N, df, SE, r, z, U, χ², η², d, ηp², R² and more

✓ Variable tracking: Detects variable names (m, n, x, y, t, z, a, b, c, Greek letters)

✓ Zero-leading format: APA & MLA enforce leading zeros (p = .05); other standards vary

✓ Style-specific rendering: Applies font, spacing, and indentation rules per citation style

Formatting Rules by Style

APA 7th Edition: Times New Roman, 2.0 line spacing, 0.5" indent, omit leading zeros

Harvard: Arial, 1.5 line spacing, 0" indent, include all zeros

Chicago: Times New Roman, 1.5 line spacing, 0.5" indent

MLA 9th: Times New Roman, 2.0 line spacing, 0.5" indent, omit leading zeros

IEEE: Times New Roman, 1.0 line spacing, justified alignment

Tips

• Large manuscripts may take a few seconds to process

• Use "Copy Corrected Manuscript" to preserve rich formatting

• Download as .doc for further editing in Microsoft Word

• All processing happens in your browser — no data is sent to any server
